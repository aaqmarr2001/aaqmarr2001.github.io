Bangladesh Facts

Introduction

Bangladesh is a huge delta region formed to the north of the Bay of Bengal, with India surrounding all 3 other borders.

1971 saw Bangladesh's independence war, which was in response to the atrocities and animosity of Pakistani troops in Bangladeshi land. In terms of area, it is 65 times smaller than China. Its industrial production growth rate is a staggering 10.2% (2017 est.). It has the longest sea beach in the world - Cox's Bazaar, and a coastline of 580km. It has a total of 18 airports. Its civil aircraft registration country code prefix is S2.

Bangladesh is the only country to have ever fought for its mother language - Bengali.
